Philip Empl received a master’s degree in management information systems from the University of Regensburg in 2020. He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. degree with the Chair of Information Systems, University of Regensburg, Germany. Since 2020, he has also been a Research Assistant with the Chair of Information Systems, University of Regensburg, Germany. There he is involved in the research project SISSeC funded by the Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi).

His research interests include the field of Internet of Things (IoT) Security. His primary focus is the instrumentation of digital twins to enhance cybersecurity in IoT environments. In doing so, he is investigating digital twins as enablers for the management and protection of IoT and as a method for attack detection and mitigation. The application scenarios of digital twins range widely, e.g., to train employees, orchestrate security mechanisms, or perform security analyses.
